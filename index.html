About Me

Researcher and lecturer specializing in Artificial Intelligence and Applied Machine Learning, with experience in cybersecurity, software engineering, and predictive modeling.

Research Experience

I completed my Ph.D. under the supervision of Naouel Moha (ETS) and Tegawende F. Bissyandé (University of Luxembourg), focusing on applied machine learning in software engineering and security. Since 2018, my research journey in computer science and artificial intelligence has been built around applied projects and interdisciplinary collaborations. 2018–2019: As a Research Assistant at UQAM (Montréal, Canada), I contributed to practical work in software engineering and Python for science. 2019–2020: As a visiting researcher at SnT(Luxembourg) I investitaged the effectiveness of timely patching detection and vulnerability-introducing patches, working with Professor Jacques Klein and Tegawende F. Bissyandé to outperforming the state-of-the-art in software vulnerabilities management. 2020–2023: Mentoring data analytics projects for professionals in Paris (Openclassrooms), using Python, R, MySQL, and Tableau. 2023–2024: During my postdoctoral research at Université Laval (Québec, Canada), I conducted applied AI research for market trend forecasting in the livestock sector, integrating multi-source data (zootechnical, genetic, traceability, and pricing) and collaborated with Eric Paquet (ULAVAL) to develop predictive models for sales, prices, and margins. Since 2024: As an Assistant Professor at Université Joseph Ki-Zerbo (IFOAD), I continue applied AI research, supervise student scientific projects, and actively contribute to new modeling and analysis approaches.

Teaching Experience

Since 2019, I have been teaching at various levels and institutions, combining pedagogy with hands-on projects. At UQAM and Teccart (Montréal, Canada, 2019–2022), I designed and delivered courses in Software Engineering, Systems Analysis and Design, and Python for Science, enabling students in economics and finance to apply data science to real-world problems. I supervised numerous student projects from design to implementation, guiding teams in deploying concrete technical solutions. Concurrently, I served as a Data Analytics Mentor in OpenClassrooms (2020–2023), training professionals from diverse backgrounds in Python, R, SQL, Knime, Tableau, and Orange, and assisting them in practical projects to transition into data-related careers. Since 2024, as an Assistant Professor at Université Joseph Ki-Zerbo (IFOAD), I have been developing new courses in Artificial Intelligence and Data Science, supervising student scientific projects, and contributing to program modernization to better prepare students for current technological challenges.
